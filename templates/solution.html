Given the system:

\[ \begin{cases} a + b + c = 4 \\ a^2 + b^2 + c^2 = 10 \\ a^3 + b^3 + c^3 = 22 \end{cases} \]

We want to find: \[ a^4 + b^4 + c^4 = ? \]

Step 1: Use Newton’s identities

  • \( S_1 = a + b + c = 4 \)
  • \( S_2 = a^2 + b^2 + c^2 = 10 \)
  • \( S_3 = a^3 + b^3 + c^3 = 22 \)
  • \( S_4 = a^4 + b^4 + c^4 = ? \)

Step 2: Express symmetric sums

Let: \[ e_1 = a + b + c = S_1 = 4, \quad e_2 = ab + bc + ca, \quad e_3 = abc \]

Using identity:

\[ S_2 = e_1^2 - 2e_2 \Rightarrow 10 = 16 - 2e_2 \Rightarrow e_2 = 3 \]

Step 3: Use Newton’s identity for \( S_3 \)

\[ S_3 = e_1 S_2 - e_2 S_1 + 3e_3 \Rightarrow 22 = 4 \cdot 10 - 3 \cdot 4 + 3e_3 \Rightarrow 22 = 28 + 3e_3 \Rightarrow e_3 = -2 \]

Step 4: Use Newton’s identity for \( S_4 \)

\[ S_4 = e_1 S_3 - e_2 S_2 + e_3 S_1 \Rightarrow S_4 = 4 \cdot 22 - 3 \cdot 10 + (-2) \cdot 4 = 88 - 30 - 8 = 50 \]

Final Answer: \( \boxed{50} \)
